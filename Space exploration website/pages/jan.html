Jan Wörner

Jan Wörner, former Director General of the European Space Agency (ESA), has been instrumental in advancing European space exploration. His vision of a collaborative "Moon Village" represents a new era of international partnership and sustainable lunar exploration, making him a significant figure in the space industry.

Key Contributions to Space Exploration

Moon Village Concept

Wörner proposed the Moon Village concept, envisioning a collaborative international effort to create a sustainable human presence on the Moon.

European Space Policy

His leadership strengthened European space policy, fostering innovation and establishing partnerships that expanded Europe’s role in global space exploration.

Mars and Beyond

Wörner supported ESA’s ExoMars mission, aiming to explore Mars and search for signs of past life, marking Europe’s ambition in interplanetary missions.

International Partnerships

Wörner fostered partnerships with space agencies worldwide, advancing collaborative projects like the International Space Station and lunar exploration.

Earth Observation Initiatives

He promoted ESA’s Earth observation programs, utilizing satellites for environmental monitoring and addressing climate change challenges.

Future Space Technologies

Wörner championed innovations in space technology, including autonomous systems and robotics, crucial for future exploration missions.
