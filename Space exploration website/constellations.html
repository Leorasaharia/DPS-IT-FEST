Zodiac Constellations

The zodiac constellations are a group of twelve star patterns that the sun passes through over the course of a year. These constellations have been used for millennia to track time and are tied to astrology.

Zodiac Constellations

Aries

Aries, the Ram, is a prominent zodiac constellation in the northern hemisphere. Its brightest star, Hamal, marks the head of the ram. Aries represents the start of the zodiac and is known for its association with leadership, courage, and energy in astrology, often symbolizing new beginnings.

Taurus

Taurus, the Bull, is a prominent zodiac constellation located between Aries and Gemini. Its brightest star, Aldebaran, represents the bull's eye. Taurus is home to the famous Pleiades star cluster and the Hyades. In astrology, it symbolizes stability, determination, and materialism, often associated with grounding energy.

Gemini

Gemini, the Twins, represents Castor and Pollux, twin brothers in Greek mythology. Located in the zodiac between Taurus and Cancer, it’s one of the brightest constellations in the sky. Gemini is known for its twin stars, Castor and Pollux, and in astrology, it represents duality, communication, and adaptability.

Cancer

Cancer, the Crab, is a faint zodiac constellation located between Gemini and Leo. Its brightest star, Acubens, marks the crab's claw. In mythology, Cancer represents the crab that harassed Heracles during his labors. Astrologically, Cancer symbolizes emotion, intuition, and nurturing qualities, often linked with home and family.

Leo

Leo, the Lion, is a prominent zodiac constellation known for its bright stars, including Regulus, which marks the lion’s heart. Located between Cancer and Virgo, it is often associated with strength, courage, and leadership. In mythology, Leo represents the Nemean Lion defeated by Heracles, embodying regal power and vitality.

Virgo

Virgo, the Virgin, is one of the largest and most recognizable zodiac constellations. Its brightest star, Spica, marks the wheat sheaf held by Virgo. In astrology, Virgo represents purity, organization, and service, often symbolizing practicality and attention to detail. It is linked with harvest and fertility in mythology.
